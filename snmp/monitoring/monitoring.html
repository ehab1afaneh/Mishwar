The simple part of the SNMP only concern the agent with its ressources limitations. The management station does not have those restrictions. A monitor is a smart, streetwise partner.

What can be done by monitors ?
  • watch a LAN as a whole.
  • gather information without polling and adding traffic.
  • track the traffic flows that contribute the most to the network activity.
  • detect failures hard to find by polling.
  • find duplicate IP address assignments.
  • it can be configured to watch for signs of troubles (because it is "intelligent" : made of a CPU, memory and hard-drive resources).
  • it could be asked to send trap message when an error counter passes over danger threshold.
  • gather and tabulate many statistics. Monitor operator can configure the monitor to show the data in the most significant way for the user.
Usually, monitors are attached to LANs, but it can watch wide area network (WAN) interfaces, track traffic and check whether errors stay within acceptable bounds. They are bought because it is invaluable for problem diagnosis.

The variable of RMON are contained in the RMON MIB .
